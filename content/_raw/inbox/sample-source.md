The Zettelkasten ("slip box") method is a note-taking system built around three
ideas:

1. Atomic notes — one idea per note, written in your own words.
2. Linking — every note connects to related notes, forming a web of thought.
3. Emergence — structure is not imposed up front; it grows from the links.

Niklas Luhmann famously used a paper Zettelkasten of ~90,000 notes to write
dozens of books. The digital equivalent is an Obsidian-style vault where notes
are markdown files and links are wikilinks.

Key practices:
- Capture raw material into an inbox, then process it deliberately.
- Give each note a descriptive title; the title is the address.
- Review and re-link periodically so the web stays dense.
